Moving Bodies & Fixtures

By default bodies and fixtures are created either at their origin or at their centroid.

Moving Bodies

Bodies can be moved around the simulation in two ways. One way, while the simulation is not running, is to manually assign the translation and rotation while creating or editing the body using the Transform tab. This is useful when you need the body to be at a specific location and rotation.



Another way, while the simulation is not running, to move a body (after it has been created) is to use the Left Mouse Button. You can click and hold the Left Mouse Button on the body (any of its fixtures) and drag the mouse around to drag the body around.

Bodies can also be rotated using the mouse. You can click and hold the Right Mouse Button on the body (any of its fixtures) and drag the mouse around to rotated the body about its center of mass.


When the simulation is running the translation and rotation of a body cannot be changed directly. Instead, it is changed using a Mouse Joint. This joint is created and destroyed automatically by the Sandbox when you click and hold the Left Mouse Button on any part of a body. The Mouse Joint allows you to drag the body around using a rubber band like rope.

This can be used in Compiled tests as well and is the only way to move bodies in those tests.

Moving Fixtures

Moving fixtures is just as easy. One way, when the simulation is not running, to move a fixture is to apply translation and rotation using the Transform tab in the Create or Edit Fixture Dialog. This does not have the same effect as it has when applied to bodies. The translation and rotation applied here directly modifies the vertices/center of the shape. As a result, when editing the Fixture you will notice that the translation and rotation are not saved.



Fixtures can also be moved, when the simulation is not running, using the mouse. Double clicking the Left Mouse Button will take you into fixture movement mode. In this mode you can use the same body moving controls above to move the individual fixtures. Clicking on an area not within the body will cancel fixture move mode.



Notes:
  • The selected body or fixture will have a blue outline.
  • Fixtures cannot be moved while the simulation is running.
  • The Mouse Joint created when the simulation is running has a maximum force that is allowed to apply.
